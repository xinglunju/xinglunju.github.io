Outflows in L1551 IRS5

 

The bipolar molecular outflows in IRS5 were discovered by Snell, Loren & Plambeck (1980). Later on, a binary (Rodriguez et al. 1998) or even perhaps a triple (Lim & Takakuwa 2006) system was found inside IRS5.


Then Wu, Takakuwa & Lim, 2009, using the sub-millimeter array (SMA), identified the splendid outflow systems of IRS5.

Above is channel maps of the SMA datacube, taken from Wu, Takakuwa & Lim, 2009. The velocity is marked on the upper-right corner in each panel, so the 12CO emission goes from blue-shifted to red-shifted with respect to the systematic velocity (~6.8 km/s) through these panels. The coordinates here are the offsets from the pointing center.


The reason we want to study the outflows in IRS5 as well as other star formation regions is that they are playing an essential role in star forming process therefore can be used as a tracer of protostars, and they themselves are also important in affecting the environment, such as feeding turbulence into the ISM.


Advanced readers interested in outflows, jets, and stellar winds can read the AY201b JC writeup, or proceed to this page on these concepts, and this page on the their relations with star formation in IRS 5.


As you can see, the kinematics in this region is complex. The authors, with their skills in isolating outflow components with the help of channel maps, P-V diagrams and Gaussian-fitting, are able to separate three bipolar outflow systems. However, for people with little experience in dealing with 3-D datacubes, it is not so intuitive to imagine the kinematics thus to find some coherent structures inside the datacube.


Therefore, this module is made to assist the understanding of 3-dimentional, Position-Position-Velocity space in which most of the radio datacubes are stored.
